# Brand Funnel Signals — Plain Language Guide

Each stage of the funnel answers one question: *how well is this brand doing at this moment in the customer journey?* The signals below are the real data we pull to answer that question. If a signal has no data, it's excluded and the remaining signals carry the full weight.

---

## Stage 1 — Awareness
**The question:** How many people have encountered this brand in the last 30 days?

| Signal | Why it's here |
|---|---|
| **Social SOV (Share of Voice)** | The share of online conversation that belongs to this brand vs competitors. If more people are talking about you than your rivals, your awareness is growing. |
| **OOH Monthly Reach** | Every active billboard, bus shelter, or screen has a daily traffic estimate. Multiply by 30 days and you get how many eyeballs physically passed the brand's outdoor presence. |
| **Digital Ad Impressions** | The total number of times your Meta and Google ads were shown to someone in the last 30 days. Raw reach — not engagement, just exposure. |
| **Event Attendance** | How many people physically attended brand events this month. Face-to-face contact is one of the strongest awareness signals in Nigerian markets. |
| **Influencer Post Reach** | The combined reach of posts by influencers tied to this brand in the last 30 days. Shows how far word-of-mouth from creators is spreading. |
| **Press / Earned Reach** | The estimated readership of news articles and media mentions in the last 90 days. Earned media = people reading about the brand without the brand paying for it. |
| **TV GRPs Delivered** | Gross Rating Points — the industry measure for how heavily a TV campaign ran. Higher GRPs mean more households saw the ad, more times. |
| **Radio Spots** | Number of radio ad slots aired in the last 30 days. Radio still reaches millions of Nigerians, especially outside Lagos and Abuja. |
| **AI Visibility Score** | How prominently the brand appears when people ask ChatGPT, Gemini, or Perplexity questions in this category. Increasingly, people "discover" brands through AI answers before they ever search Google. |

---

## Stage 2 — Consideration
**The question:** Are people actively engaging with or seeking out this brand?

| Signal | Why it's here |
|---|---|
| **Social Engagement Rate** | The average likes, comments, and shares per post relative to reach. High engagement means people aren't just seeing the content — they're reacting to it. That reaction is consideration. |
| **Consideration-tagged Content** | Posts that our system has classified as driving consideration (comparisons, "thinking about buying", "heard about this brand"). This shows organic intent signals in the content itself. |
| **Brand Mention Volume** | How many times the brand was mentioned in the last 30 days (excluding competitor mentions). Volume of conversation is a proxy for how much people are thinking and talking about you. |
| **Digital CTR (Click-Through Rate)** | The percentage of people who saw a digital ad and clicked it. Clicking is an active choice — it means the person wanted to know more. That's the definition of consideration. |
| **Video View-Through Rate** | The percentage of viewers who watched the video ad to the end. Completing a video is a strong signal of genuine interest, not passive scrolling. |
| **AI Visibility Score** | Reused from Awareness here because people also "consider" brands through AI — asking "what's the best food brand in Nigeria?" and seeing your brand ranked well drives consideration. |

---

## Stage 3 — Preference
**The question:** Do people have a positive opinion of this brand over alternatives?

| Signal | Why it's here |
|---|---|
| **Sentiment Score (14-day avg)** | The brand's average social sentiment score over the last two weeks, on a 0–100 scale. This directly answers: do people feel good or bad about this brand right now? |
| **Positive Sentiment % (14-day)** | The share of social mentions that were positive in tone. Separate from the score — a brand can have "neutral" average sentiment but still have 80% positive mentions. |
| **Perception Quality** | Average score from perception audit surveys (questions on quality, trust, value, innovation, etc.). This is what customers say directly when asked, not just inferred from social. |
| **Cultural Resonance** | A composite score measuring how well the brand connects with local culture — language, values, occasions, humour. In Nigerian markets, cultural fit is often what tips preference. |
| **Press Sentiment** | The share of media articles that are positive in tone. When journalists and bloggers write favourably, it reinforces the brand's reputation in people's minds. |
| **Marketplace Own Rating** | The average star rating of the brand's own products on marketplace platforms. A 4.5-star product vs a 3-star competitor is one of the clearest preference signals that exists. |

---

## Stage 4 — Action
**The question:** Are people actually buying, converting, or taking decisive steps?

| Signal | Why it's here |
|---|---|
| **Purchase Events** | The count of completed purchases recorded in the last 30 days. This is the most direct action signal there is. |
| **E-commerce Sales (units)** | Units sold through connected e-commerce channels. Even one unit sold means someone took action. |
| **Digital Conversions** | Conversions tracked across Meta and Google campaigns — purchases, sign-ups, form fills, whatever the campaign goal was set to. |
| **Event Lead Capture** | The ratio of event interactions that resulted in a lead or new customer. If 10% of people who stopped at your event stand gave their details, that's a strong action signal. |
| **OOH Visit-throughs** | Visits tracked via the branded vanity URLs on outdoor ads. Someone typing that URL into their phone is taking a deliberate action off the back of seeing an outdoor ad. |
| **Referral Conversions** | The total conversions credited to referral codes. When a person shares a referral link and their friend converts, both parties took action. |
| **SDK Conversions** | Purchase and lead events recorded through our mobile SDK. Captures in-app and mobile-web actions that don't go through the usual ad platforms. |

---

## Stage 5 — Loyalty
**The question:** Do customers keep coming back and feel good about the relationship?

| Signal | Why it's here |
|---|---|
| **NPS Score** | Net Promoter Score from actual post-purchase surveys. NPS = (promoters − detractors) ÷ total. It's the gold standard for measuring loyalty and likelihood to recommend. Rescaled from −100/+100 to 0–100 for the funnel. |
| **Repeat Customer Rate** | The share of customer profiles who have placed 2 or more orders. If 40% of customers come back, that's a loyalty story. If 5% do, it's a retention problem. |
| **Retention Health** | Inverted average retention risk score (100 − avg risk). Our system flags customers at risk of churning; this signal shows how healthy the base is overall. |
| **Loyalty Program Activity** | The count of loyalty earn transactions in the last 30 days. Active loyalty programme = customers engaging with the incentive to stay. |
| **Post-purchase Sentiment (7d)** | The average sentiment score from the last 7 days specifically. After someone buys, are people online happy or disappointed? A sharp drop here often signals a fulfilment or product quality issue. |

---

## Stage 6 — Advocacy
**The question:** Are loyal customers actively recommending the brand to others?

| Signal | Why it's here |
|---|---|
| **Advocacy Score (weekly)** | A composite score calculated weekly by our advocacy engine — combines promoter behaviour, referral activity, and share patterns into one number. |
| **Referral Activity** | Combined total of referral link events (sign-ups and purchases) plus referral code conversions in the last 30 days. Direct evidence that customers are bringing in other customers. |
| **Active Promoters** | The count of people enrolled in the brand's promoter programme with active status. These are people who have raised their hand to champion the brand. |
| **NPS Promoter Share** | The percentage of recent NPS respondents who scored 9 or 10 (promoters). People who give you a 9 or 10 are statistically very likely to recommend you. This is separate from the NPS score — it isolates the enthusiast base. |
| **Visual Brand Advocacy** | The count of photos and videos (from visual listening) that show the brand positively or neutrally in the last 30 days. When customers are posting photos of your product without being asked, that is pure advocacy. |
| **Organic Share Rate** | The ratio of shares to total engagements on social posts. Shares are the most valuable engagement action because they extend reach to the sharer's own network — at zero cost to the brand. |

---

## How the scoring works

Each signal has a **scale** — the value that maps to a perfect score of 100. For example:
- 500,000 digital impressions → 10 out of 100 (scale is 5 million)
- A 4.5/5 marketplace rating → 90 out of 100 (scale is 5.0)
- 100% positive press → 100 out of 100

Each signal also has a **base weight**. If a brand has no TV presence, the TV signal is simply excluded and the other signals share its weight — so the score still makes sense even for brands with no offline media spend.

The final stage score is the weighted average of all active signal scores.
